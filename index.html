THE PROBLEM

Most Retail Options Traders
Lose Money. Here's Why.

It's almost never because they picked the wrong stock. It's because they built the wrong system around it.

KILLER 01

They Buy, Not Sell

75-85% of bought options expire worthless. New traders default to buying calls because it feels cheap. Selling premium flips the math in your favor.

KILLER 02

They Trade Too Often

Every extra hour in front of a screen is another chance to make an emotional decision. Day traders don't lose to the market. They lose to themselves.

KILLER 03

They Skip The Filter

No universe of vetted names. No rules on implied volatility. No line on account allocation. One bad name on an earnings week wipes out six months of wins.

THE FRAMEWORK

Set And Done,
Built In 3 Pillars

PILLAR 1

Selection

A filtered universe of roughly 25-40 S&P names. Liquidity, volatility band, earnings calendar, channel state. If it doesn't pass the filter, I don't touch it.

PILLAR 2

Structure

One spread setup handles 80% of my trades. Cash-secured puts on companies I'd happily own at a discount. Either outcome pays. Neither requires a prediction.

PILLAR 3

Schedule

Trades placed Monday morning. Closed or rolled Friday afternoon. No screen time in between. The cadence is what makes this sustainable over years, not months.

FAQ

Common Questions

Do I need trading experience?

No. The Mastermind is designed for beginner and intermediate investors. I expect you to paper trade the framework for 30 days before going live with real capital. That protects you and the framework.

What account size do I need?

Most students start between $10,000 and $50,000 of investable capital. Some start with $6,000 and scale in. Some start with $200,000. The framework adapts to the account. We'll size it on the call.

How much time will this take per week?

Roughly 30 minutes Monday morning and 15 minutes Friday afternoon. Plus the weekly group call. No screen time between Monday and Friday unless something major happens in the market.

Is this like day trading or signal services?

No, and intentionally so. Day trading is a full-time job with a losing expected value for retail. Signal services make you dependent on someone else's picks. Set And Done teaches you a framework you can run yourself for the next 20 years.

Who is this NOT for?

People looking for overnight returns. People who cannot paper trade for 30 days before going live. People who want a signal service. If you want adrenaline, you're on the wrong page. This is built for compounding, not gambling.
